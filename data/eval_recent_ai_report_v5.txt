Biggest Announcements

Recent developments in AI have been marked by significant advancements in hardware, software, and applications. The following are some of the most notable announcements:

* On December 18, 2025, a study published in Nature found that researchers at Tsinghua University developed an optical feature extraction engine (OFE2) that processes data at 12.5 GHz using light, potentially leading to breakthroughs in AI computing speed.
* In November 2025, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) Center for Constructive Communication announced a new method developed at MIT could root out vulnerabilities and improve large language model (LLM) safety and performance.
* Google partnered with five American electricity providers in December 2025 to reduce power consumption at its data centers during peak demand, highlighting the growing importance of sustainable AI practices.
* In October 2025, researchers at the Optical Society presented the development of an optical processor, Breakthrough Optical Processor, that can compute at the speed of light, offering potential for AI applications.
* Recent findings from the MIT Center for Constructive Communication suggest that leading AI models perform worse for users with lower English proficiency, less formal education, and non-US origins. Researchers are now working on addressing these disparities.

These announcements highlight the rapid progress being made in various areas of AI research and development, with a focus on improving efficiency, safety, and inclusivity.
